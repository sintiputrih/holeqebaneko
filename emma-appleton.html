    Who is Emma Appleton?

    Emma Appleton is a talented British actress from Oxford, England. Before getting into acting, she worked in the modelling industry. When Emma was in school, she enjoyed drama, which is why she got into the acting field. She starred in a short movie named Dreamland for the first time and never looked back. The actress is renowned for acting in The Witcher’s popular Netflix show, Princess Renfri. People love Emma’s performance in The Witcher.

    Biography TimeLine

    Emma Appleton is an English actress who has made a name for herself in the entertainment industry. She has starred in several movies and TV shows, earning critical acclaim for her performances. Emma started acting in 2016 and has since worked on various projects. Critics and audiences have praised her diverse roles and ability to immerse herself into characters. With her talent and dedication, Emma is expected to continue making strides in her acting career in the years to come.

    • 2013: Emma graduates from the Oxford School of Drama.
    • 2016: Emma makes her acting debut in the British drama series “Clique,” in which she plays the role of Fay Brookstone. The show airs on BBC Three.
    • 2017: Emma appears in the horror movie “The Dreamer,” directed by Lauren Cooney. She also stars in the British television series “Grantchester,” playing the role of Felicity Law.
    • 2018: Emma appears in the BBC One drama series “The Trial of Christine Keeler,” portraying the character of Mandy Rice-Davies. She also appears in the Netflix thriller series “The End of the F***ing World” as Beth.
    • 2019: Emma plays Princess Renfri in the first season of the Netflix series “The Witcher.”
    • 2020: Emma stars as Anna in the Apple TV+ series “Trying,” a comedy-drama about a couple trying to have a baby.
    • 2021: Emma appears in the British series “The Pursuit of Love,” based on the novel by Nancy Mitford, playing the role of Linda Radlett. She also stars in the Apple TV+ series “The Mosquito Coast,” based on the novel by Paul Theroux.
