    The model and My Body author has impeccable interior taste

      As if we weren't envious of Emily Ratajkowski already, the supermodel goes and shares a glimpse inside her serene New York home which, quite simply, is divine. The star lives with her son Sylvester in the sun-swathed apartment in the Big Apple, and we can't help but wonder if Harry Styles himself has paid the pad a visit post-kiss.

      Following her split from her husband Sebastian Bear-McClard, the 31-year-old downsized and moved and into a modern flat that boasts her signature retro interior touch. On Thursday, Emrata shared a brand-new glimpse inside the beautifully designed apartment.

      Shooting a campaign with Emily posed for a series of photographs alongside toddler Sly that showed off her artistic taste in décor.

      The living room

      View post on Instagram 

      Emily's living room is lined with the most exquisite abstract works of art that perfectly matched baby Sylvester's painterly-printed sweatshirt. The contemporary pieces punctuated the white walls of the room and were lined by a white chest of drawers, bubble-effect bases, a velvet sofa and full-length mirrors for optimum selfie opportunities.

      The terrace

      Basking in the sunshine, the mother-of-one offered up a look at her rustic outdoor area. White-washed walls encased large glass windows that allowed the East Coast light to flood the interior space, while sprawling vines bedecked the black outdoor furniture – adding a fairytale element to her bachelorette pad.

      The bedroom

      Emily opted for a minimalist aesthetic when it came to designing her bedroom. The model championed a blank canvas approach to decorating, choosing crisp white bed sheets and white walls for maximum space serenity. The star houses her makeup bag beside her bed, which is also a source of a great deal of fun for her angelic child.

      Emily sold her previous Los Angeles home for $2.5 million - a house that she shared with her ex Sebastian. Her new house certainly echoes the style of the author's former residence, yet there are pieces she has seemingly left behind.
