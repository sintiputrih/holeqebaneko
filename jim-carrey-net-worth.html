    What is Jim Carrey’s net worth and salary?

    Jim Carrey is a Canadian-American actor, comedian, and producer with a net worth of $180 million. He was the most famous actor in the world for about a decade and regularly one of the highest-paid actors in the world, earning a base salary of $20+ million for a single movie. Jim Carrey was the first actor to earn $20 million for a movie and has earned at least $300 million to date from film salaries and bonuses.

    Carrey started his career as a stand-up comedian in Canada and caught the eye of Rodney Dangerfield in the late 70s. He moved to California and began to gain higher profile roles in films like “Earth Girls Are Easy” and “Peggy Sue Got Married.” In 1990, he started appearing on the series “In Living Color” which introduced him to an even wider audience. In 1994, he landed the lead role in the massive hit film “Ace Ventura: Pet Detective” and dominated comedic film for the next ten years.

    During this time, Carrey starred in a string of popular projects such as “The Mask,” “Dumb and Dumber,” “Liar Liar,” and “Bruce Almighty,” as well as a number of films that blurred the line between comedy and drama, including “The Truman Show,” “Man on the Moon,” and “Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind.”

    Jim Carrey: One of the Highest-Paid Entertainers in the World

    Jim Carrey consistently ranked as one of the highest-paid entertainers worldwide in the late 1990s and 2000s. He made history by becoming the first actor to earn $20 million for a single film with “The Cable Guy” in 1996. Throughout his career, Carrey has earned hundreds of millions of dollars from film salaries and backend bonuses.

    Early Life and Career

    Jim Carrey was born in Newmarket, Ontario, Canada on January 17, 1962. His parents were a homemaker and an accountant. When Jim was 12, his father lost his job and the family became homeless. They lived out of a van and worked overnight as janitors and security guards to make ends meet. Jim started doing stand-up comedy in Toronto clubs at the age of 15, and dropped out of high school to pursue his career. He later moved to Los Angeles, where he was signed by Rodney Dangerfield as an opening act.

    Jim was always a natural comedian and impersonator, following in the footsteps of his father. In his early days, his father would drive him to clubs. After a full day of school, Jim would frequently work a full eight-hour shift at a nearby factory.

    Jim Carrey’s Legendary Check

    In 1985, a broke and depressed Jim Carrey drove his beat-up Toyota Camry into the Hollywood Hills and wrote himself a check for $10 million “for acting services rendered”. He post-dated it 10 years later and kept the check in his wallet.

    Within that 10 year period, Jim’s dream came true and he became a successful actor. In 1994, when Jim’s father died, he slipped the check into his father’s casket.

    Jim Carrey’s Rise to Fame

    Jim Carrey’s first taste of stardom came in 1990 on the Wayans brothers’ sketch comedy show “In Living Color”. On the show, he wowed audiences with characters like Fire Marshal Bill. Carrey remained on In Living Color until it ended in 1995, but shortly before its cancellation, his major movie debut “Ace Ventura: Pet Detective” hit theaters. The movie was a smash hit that made him a household name overnight. The same year, he starred in “The Mask” and “Dumb and Dumber”, both of which were box-office hits.

    For his next film, “The Cable Guy”, he earned $20 million, setting a salary record. Carrey’s other notable comedic vehicles include “Liar, Liar”, “Bruce Almighty”, “Me, Myself, and Irene”, and “Yes Man”. He has also appeared in a number of dramas and dramedies, including “The Truman Show”, “Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind”, “The Majestic”, “Man on the Moon”, and the thriller “The Number 23”.

    Jim Carrey’s Lucrative Film Career

    Jim Carrey’s first major paycheck came from his role in In Living Color, earning $25,000 per episode. He went on to earn millions from his successful film career.

    For his role in Ace Ventura, Jim was paid $350,000, but after the movie earned $100 million, he was paid $15 million for the sequel. He also earned $540,000 for his role in The Mask, which made $350 million, and $7 million for Dumb & Dumber.

    Jim made history as the first actor to earn a $20 million salary for a single film, which he accomplished with The Cable Guy in 1996. He continued to earn $20 million for several other films, including How the Grinch Stole Christmas.

    Jim’s success continued with Yes Man, where he earned a total payday of $35 million thanks to a percentage of profits. Between 1994 and 2008 alone, Jim earned $200 million from salaries and bonuses, and he’s likely earned over $300 million in film salary to date.

    Jim Carrey’s Marriages and Relationships

    Jim Carrey has been married twice. He was married to Melissa Womer from 1987 to 1995. They have a daughter together named Jane Erin Carrey who was a contestant on American Idol in 2012.

    In 1996, Jim married his Dumb & Dumber co-star Lauren Holly. The marriage lasted less than a year.

    Between 2005 and 2010 he dated model/actress Jenny McCarthy.

    Jim Carrey’s Awards and Accolades

    Jim Carrey has won over 40 major awards throughout his career, including two Golden Globe Awards for Best Performance by an Actor in a Motion Picture. He won his first Golden Globe in 1999 for his role in The Truman Show, and his second in 2000 for his portrayal of Andy Kaufman in Man on the Moon.

    Jim Carrey’s Real Estate Holdings

    Jim Carrey owns several properties in the United States, including his longtime primary residence in the Brentwood area of Los Angeles. He purchased the first property in the compound in 1994 for $3.8 million, which now features a large guest house, swimming pool, tennis court, and approximately 300 feet of fencing and hedges around the two-acre compound with 13,000 square feet of living space. In February 2023, he listed the Brentwood estate for $28.9 million.

    Additionally, in 2002, he purchased a beachfront mansion in Malibu for $9.75 million. He listed the home for sale in 2011 for $18 million and sold it in 2013 for $13.4 million. He also owns an apartment in New York City.
