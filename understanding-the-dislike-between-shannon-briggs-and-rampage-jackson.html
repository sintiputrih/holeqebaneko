    Shannon Briggs and Rampage Jackson are two prominent figures in the world of combat sports. Their recent interactions and public statements have hinted at a mutual dislike, prompting curiosity about the reasons behind their apparent animosity. Let's delve into the possible factors contributing to their strained relationship.

    Past Exchanges and Criticisms

    Annoyance with Catchphrase Overuse

    • Quinton Jackson expressed his annoyance with Shannon Briggs' excessive use of the catchphrase "let's go, champ!" during a conversation with Ariel Helwani.
    • Jackson emphasized that while he doesn't hate Briggs, he finds him annoying and believes that he says "let's go, champ!" way too much, questioning how Briggs' family copes with it.

    Mutual War of Words

    • Both athletes have engaged in a war of words in recent times, with Briggs expressing his desire to fight Jackson in a boxing match and even mentioning the possibility of facing him under MMA rules.
    • Jackson, on the other hand, issued a stern warning to Briggs, highlighting his age and inactivity, and expressing confidence in being able to confront Briggs in the ring.

    Potential Motivations for Dislike

    Competitiveness and Ego

    • Both Briggs and Jackson have achieved significant success in their respective careers, making them highly competitive individuals. This competitiveness might have led to clashes of ego, contributing to their dislike for each other.

    Professional Rivalry

    • Given Briggs' and Jackson's backgrounds in boxing and MMA respectively, there might be an underlying professional rivalry between the two. Their potential crossover into each other's sport through a boxing or MMA match could have intensified their rivalry.

    Personal Differences

    • It's conceivable that personal differences or clashes in personality have contributed to the strained relationship between Briggs and Jackson. Their contrasting personas and approaches to public interactions could have led to friction.

    Impact of Recent Events

    Triad Combat Event

    • The Triller Triad Combat event, where Quinton Jackson and Shannon Briggs served as captains for Team MMA and Team Boxing respectively, provided a platform for their rivalry to play out publicly.
    • Their presence at the event, despite not competing against each other, indicates the lingering tension between the two athletes.

    Social Media Interactions

    • Jackson's Instagram account is mentioned in a comment, highlighting his posts "clowning" Briggs, indicating that their dislike may also manifest through social media interactions.

    the dislike between Shannon Briggs and Rampage Jackson appears to stem from a combination of factors, including annoyance with catchphrase overuse, mutual war of words, competitiveness and ego, professional rivalry, personal differences, and recent events such as the Triad Combat event and social media interactions. While the exact root cause of their animosity remains subjective, it is evident that their interactions have resulted in a strained relationship that has garnered public attention within the combat sports community.
