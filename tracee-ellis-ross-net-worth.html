    Tracee Ellis Ross is an award-winning American actress, producer, presenter, and former model.

    Ellis Ross has carved out a place for herself in the glittery world of show business. She has made a name for herself throughout the course of her 20-year career.

    She has created a reputation for herself in the fashion industry, as well as in films, TV shows, and music videos.

    However, because of her numerous qualities and perseverance, fame begins to pound on her door. She is the first African-American to be awarded a Golden Globe

    Tracee Ellis Ross Biography: Age, height, birthplace, parents, siblings, education

    Tracee Ellis Ross was born on October 29, 1972, in Los Angeles, California to parents Diana Ross and music manager Robert Ellis Silberstein.

    Rhonda Ross Kendrick and Chudney Lane Silberstein are her sisters, while Ross Arne and Evan Olav are her half-brothers. She also has three step-siblings from her mother’s marriage: Katinka, Christoffer, and Leona Naess.

    She went to Riverdale Country School in the Bronx as a kid. She then studied at the Institut Le Rosey in Switzerland. She then attended Brown University, where she majored in theatre.

    She is 49 years of age and has a height of 5 feet 7 inches (170 cm or 1.70 m).

    Tracee Ellis Ross family: Is she married? Does Tracee Ellis Ross have children?

    Tracee Ellis Ross has never married and is still unattached. However, Evan Ross, Tracee’s half-brother, is married to Ashlee Simpson, making Tracee her sister-in-law.

    Tracee Ellis Ross TV shows and awards

    She received a BET Award in 2005 for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series for the popular series Girlfriends. She received two NAACP Awards for her role in Girlfriends. 

    She won the award for Outstanding Actress in a Comedy Series in both 2007 and 2009.

    In 2012, she received the NAACP Award in the ‘Outstanding Actress in Comedy Series’ category in the series Reed Between the Lines.

    For her work in Black-ish, she received the NAACP Award in the category of Outstanding Actress in a Comedy Series in 2015 and 2016.

    In 2017, she received the Golden Globe Award in the Best Actress – Television Series Musical or Comedy’ category for her performance

    Some great shows of Ellis include; Girlfriends, Black-ish, Far Harbor (1996), Sue(1997) and A Fare to Remember (1999).

    She also took on hosting duties of the Lifetime talk show The Dish for a year (1997) and appeared in a few more movies at the turn of the century, including Hanging Up and In the Weeds
