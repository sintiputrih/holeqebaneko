    Bob Odenkirk’s Net Worth as of 2023

    According to Celebrity Net Worth, Bob Odenkirk has a net worth of $16 million as of 2023. This is a result of his successful career in various fields of entertainment, especially his leading role on Better Call Saul. Odenkirk is not only the star of the show, but also one of its producers. He reportedly earns $200,000 per episode for the sixth and final season of the series

    Bob Odenkirk’s Career Highlights

    Bob Odenkirk started his career as a comedy writer for Saturday Night Live in 1987. He worked with other writers such as Conan O’Brien and Robert Smigel on many sketches. He also performed in a few skits on the show

    In 1995, he created his own sketch comedy show with David Cross, called Mr. Show with Bob and David. The show aired on HBO for four seasons and received critical acclaim. Odenkirk and Cross also wrote and starred in a movie based on the show, called Run Ronnie Run1.

    Odenkirk then transitioned to more dramatic roles, starting with his appearance as Saul Goodman on Breaking Bad in 2009. He played the character for five seasons, becoming a fan favorite and a key part of the story. He also reprised his role in the prequel series Better Call Saul, which focuses on his character’s backstory and transformation. The show has been nominated for multiple Emmy awards, including Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series for Odenkirk

    Besides Breaking Bad and Better Call Saul, Odenkirk has also appeared in many other TV shows and movies, such as Fargo, The Post, Little Women, Nobody, and The Disaster Artist. He has also directed and produced several projects, such as The Birthday Boys, W/ Bob & David, Girlfriend’s Day, and Let’s Go to Prison

    Odenkirk is also an established voice actor, lending his voice to animated shows such as The Simpsons, BoJack Horseman, Bob’s Burgers, and The Incredibles 2

    Bob Odenkirk’s Personal and Early Life

    Bob Odenkirk was born on October 22nd of 1962 in Berwyn, Illinois. He grew up in Naperville with six siblings in a Catholic household. His parents divorced when he was young due to his father’s alcoholism1.

    He graduated from high school at the age of 16 and attended several colleges in Illinois. He majored in radio broadcasting and created his own late-night comedy show for Southern Illinois University. He moved to Chicago before graduating from SIU and joined various comedy groups and workshops1.

    He married Naomi Yomtov in 1997 and they have two children together: Nathan and Erin1.
