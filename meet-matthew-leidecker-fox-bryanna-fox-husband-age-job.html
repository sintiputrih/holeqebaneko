    Meet Matthew Leidecker Fox, Criminologist Bryanna Fox Ex Husband

    Criminologist Bryanna Fox is very reserved when it comes to her married life. Furthermore, her virtually inactive presence on social media makes it even harder to unravel information about her marriage and relationship. However, we’ve discovered that she is currently married.

    Criminologist Bryanna Fox’s ex-husband is named Matthew Leidecker Fox. Almost all of the information regarding their relationship and marriage is shrouded in mystery. They haven’t shared for how long they dated each other before getting engaged and married.

    According to Tampa Bay Florida, Matthew and Bryanna purchased a property in January 2014 that was valued at $209,900. So, they have been married as early as 2014.

    Bryanna is currently appearing on Hulu’s The Lesson Is Murder. Over the years, Hulu has brought many true-crime-related shows that captured viewers’ attention but their newest offering The Lesson Is Murder is a show where Bryanna Fox delves into the mind of three convicted murderers: Will Davis, Robert Fratta, and Ivié DeMolina with her students.

    Bryanna is a criminologist, former FBI Special Agent, and associate professor. “This idea for this show was inspired by my 2018 forensic psychology class at USF,” Fox said to USF News. “My students ended up breaking open that cold case, and spoiler alert, something similar happens with a case in the first episode of this docuseries.”

    Bryanna is an Associate Professor in the Department of Criminology and a Faculty Affiliate of the Florida Mental Health Institute. She is a former FBI Special Agent who received her Ph.D. from Cambridge University.

    Her work focuses on developing evidence-based police and crime prevention tactics, as well as identifying psychological and developmental risk factors for criminal conduct and prolific offending. In journals including the Journal of Research in Crime and Delinquency, Justice Quarterly, Criminal Justice & Behavior, Law & Society Review, and Psychology Bulletin, she has published more than 40 peer-reviewed studies.

    Bryanna is Co-Editor of Justice Quarterly and a member of Editorial Boards for Criminology & Public Policy, Criminal Justice & Behavior, Youth Violence & Juvenile Justice, Policing: An International Journal, and the Journal of Criminal Justice. She is the founder and director of the SPRUCE Research Lab and is on the executive board of the Division of Developmental and Life-Course Criminology of the American Society of Criminology.

    In addition to the 2019 Outstanding Research Achievement Award from the University of South Florida, the 2014 Nigel Walker Prize for Outstanding Ph.D. Research from the University of Cambridge, and the 2013 Excellence in Law Enforcement Research Award from the International Association of Chiefs of Police, she also won the 2017 Early Career Award from the American Society of Criminology’s Division of Developmental and Life-Course Criminology.

    Bryanna holds B.S. from Penn State University in Law & Justice, Psychology. Find out more about her career here.
