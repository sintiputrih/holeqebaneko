    Theater Roots to Hollywood Heights: Spencer Macpherson Acting Journey

    As a child, he performed in many theater productions, showcasing his natural talent and love for the craft, and through a musical theatre workshop in Toronto, Spencer met his agent.

    After signing with an agent at the tender age of 12, Macpherson landed his first professional gig, doing voice acting for a cartoon. A coveted role followed this in a toy commercial, which helped to launch his career even further.

    In 2014, Spencer started his acting journey on the big screen by taking on the role of Hunter Hollingsworth in the popular television series “Degrassi: The Next Generation.” He worked on the show for a year until 2015, gaining valuable experience and exposure in Hollywood.

    During that same year, he also debuted in two series, “Defiance” and “Degrassi: Don’t Look Back,” by which his talent and dedication to his craft quickly became evident, and he began to establish himself in the acting world.

    After working in “Degrassi: Don’t Look Back,” Spencer’s acting journey continued to progress steadily for several months because soon his life would change forever when in 2015, he landed the role of Charles de Valois in the hit series “Reign.”

    The following year, Macpherson once again returned to his role as Hunter Hollingsworth in the spin-off series “Degrassi: Next Class,” which further cemented his fame and success in the entertainment industry. Almost overnight, he became a household name, and his popularity soared to new heights.

    As his success grew with each new project, Spencer’s star continued to rise, and soon he found himself in high demand; that’s why he got the chance to work in “Lullaby,” “Secrets at the Inn,” “Retrograde,” “Midnight Marathon,” and many others film and series.
